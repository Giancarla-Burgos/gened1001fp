The Truth Comes to Light

It started with a knock at the door.

You weren’t expecting anyone, not this late. Your son was upstairs, headphones on, pretending the world didn’t exist. When you opened the door, there he was eyes wide, voice trembling.

“I’m sorry for coming here,” he said. “But he hasn’t told you, has he?”

It didn’t take long for the truth to come to light. The stranger wasn’t a stranger. He was the one your son loved, and the one your son had given his ticket to.

“I tried to talk him out of it. He said he couldn’t let me die. I'm the one registered and I'd like to be with him for these last few moments”

You wanted to scream. Instead, you stood frozen. Because what do you do when your child sacrifices himself for someone the world won’t even recognize as worthy of a seat? The moment you laid eyes on him, it was obvious he was a Korinthian.
