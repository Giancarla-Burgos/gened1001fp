Reflection Room: Part II

You bear the guilt of killing your spouse. Your children will be left behind. What remains now is not choice—but what you can live with.
Yourself
You survived. But was it instinct... or calculation?
Son
He resents you. Distant. Quiet. Still grieving.
Daughter
Furious. Once soft-spoken. Now unrelenting.
Sometimes the virus isn’t the enemy—it’s the excuse. You remember who they were. The way your spouse hummed in the kitchen. The way your children clung to your sleeves. Memory fragments, once comfort, now questions. Who are you when no one left knows you? Are you still you?
