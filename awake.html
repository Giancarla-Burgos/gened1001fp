You Stayed Awake

From the hallway, you watched the soft hum of the kitchen light flicker to life. Your spouse stood in front of the drawer — the drawer. The one no one else knew about.

The family's sole ticket glinted in their hand before they even opened it fully. Like they'd rehearsed it.

No pause. No second thought. They held it up to the light like a passport to a future only they were meant to reach. You could barely hear them, but the words cut clean:

“I’m sorry. Someone had to choose.”

You didn’t move. Not at first. You let the silence expand between you, like a waiting room with no answers. Then, slowly, you stepped forward.

“How dare you.”

Their eyes found yours. No anger. No excuses. Just a weary resignation. The kind that comes from decisions already made long before they were spoken aloud.

You didn’t scream. You didn’t argue. The house stayed quiet, like it didn’t want to know what would happen next.

By the time the sun crept through the blinds, the only thing left moving was your breath.

The ticket was warm in your palm. The body beneath you wasn’t.
