Aftermath: Fractured

The house is still. But it’s not quiet — not really. The quiet has weight now. It hangs from the ceilings, sinks into the walls, settles into your bones.

Your children walk the halls like strangers. The daughter won’t meet your eyes. She avoids the kitchen, the place where it happened. The son… he barely speaks. When he does, it’s clipped. Functional. Distant.

They know. Maybe not all of it, but enough.

At dinner, no one asks where she is. No one dares. Not even you.

You buried the truth with her, but the silence won’t stay in the ground. Every sound — the creak of the floor, the beep of the stove, the wind against the glass — feels like her ghost coming back to ask, "Why?"
