You Chose Your Daughter

She was always the kindest hearted. Even when things were already crumbling, she kept offering, listening, apologizing for things that weren’t her fault to her community.

You found her lockbox after she boarded. Beneath her research notes and worn-out textbooks, there was a letter she never meant you to read. It wasn’t addressed to anyone. Just thoughts on paper, folded neatly like something sacred.

“If I go,” it began, “I’ll try to help build something better. Perfect is unrealistic. Just better is enough. I hope, wherever I go, my family finds peace, even if I never see them again.”

She didn’t ask for salvation. She just asked for a chance to do some good. And that, more than anything, is why she was one you could live with saving.
