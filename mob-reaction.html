The Turning Point

It wasn’t supposed to happen like this. You told yourself it wouldn’t. That people would understand. That they’d see what he did as love.

But from the moment you opened your door in broad daylight, it didn’t take long. Not in a place where belief spreads faster than reason. The church bells rang that night, but not for a sermon. Just a signal.

You saw the torchlight first. Then the shouting. By the time you processed it all, they already knocked down your door. A teacher, someone who once gave your elementary-aged son candy when he got straight As, screamed first.

“He gave it up. For him. For a sickness.”

Your son's lover stepped outside. Hands up. Eyes steady. He didn’t run. He just looked at you, like he already knew. Like he was pleading for your forgiveness.

Because the moment they laid eyes on you all together, they decided what had to be down.

It would all be quiet soon.
