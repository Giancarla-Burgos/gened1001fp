Zombie Virus Symptoms, Causes, Cures

Last updated: April XX, XXXX

Overview

"Zombie Virus" is a newly documented viral disease originating in Springfield. While the official name is still under review by national health agencies, early symptoms have drawn public attention for their severity, most notably impacting psychological senses.

Common Symptoms

  • Widespread rashes on the skin
  • Persistent fatigue
  • Nausea
  • Discolored patches of skin
  • Severe migraines
  • Delayed motor coordination
  • Hallucinations or altered perception
  • Short-term memory loss
  • Unprovoked aggression
  • Low body temperature

Possible Causes

Researchers believe the virus may be zoonotic in nature, with possible links to infected mammalian rodents. Transmission has not yet been identified via airborne particles or blood contact, but further testing is ongoing.

Treatment & Containment

There is currently no known cure for the virus. Early intervention may include:

  • Isolation of affected individuals
  • Neurological sedatives for severe aggression
  • Antiviral injections (experimental)

Advisory

All travelers are advised to avoid Springfield. Contact with newly infected subjects should be reported to local health authorities immediately. Individuals experiencing symptoms are urged to self-isolate and seek medical testing.
